Tableau data analysis on Capetown Airbnb

In this project, I collected data from the Airbnb site specific to Cape Town, comprising 12 months of calendar archives and listing information. To begin, I used Excel to combine and clean the various datasets, ensuring that the tables were ready for analysis. Next, I imported the cleaned data into Tableau Public, where I conducted joins between the listing and calendar data. The join utilized was an inner join based on the listing ID.

With the data prepared and merged, I proceeded to create several insightful visuals that I later combined into a dashboard. The visuals included "Average Price per Neighborhood," "Revenue per Year," "Average Price per Number of Bedrooms," and a map showcasing listings across different neighborhoods and their corresponding average prices. This allowed me to identify which areas were more costly and in higher demand, enabling me to assess the competition.

Furthermore, I generated a count of distinct bedroom listings to gain a better understanding of the distribution of available bedrooms. This comprehensive analysis provided valuable insights into the Airbnb market in Cape Town and helped uncover pricing trends and demand patterns, facilitating better decision-making for future listings.

Excel Project On Bike Sales

In this project I worked on a public data set on a bike sale shop. The data consisted of buyer’s information such as marital status, if they have children their educational level, if they have cars or own houses and their occupations. Everything on this project was entirely done on Microsoft excel. We cleaned data then created a number of pivot tables that we later on converted into insightful visuals some questions asked was what gender and age group tend to buy bikes more than others we wanted to know their incomes and region they originated from and if the fact that they own cars and house as well as having children influenced the decision to buy a bike

Data Cleaning: To ensure the accuracy and reliability of our analysis, the first step involved data cleaning. This process aimed to rectify any errors, inconsistencies, or missing values present in the dataset. By thoroughly cleaning the data, we could proceed to the subsequent stages with confidence.

Pivot Table Creation: After cleaning the data, we created a set of pivot tables. Pivot tables allowed us to efficiently summarize and aggregate the data, providing valuable insights into different aspects of bike sales.

Insightful Visualizations: To present the findings in a more intuitive and visually appealing manner, we transformed the pivot table results into insightful visualizations. Utilizing various charts, graphs, and plots, we were able to effectively communicate patterns and trends within the data.

Key Questions Addressed: Throughout the analysis, we sought to answer several key questions: 1. Gender and Age Group Preferences: We investigated which gender and age groups showed a higher propensity for buying bikes. By analyzing the data, we could identify any preferences or variations in bike purchases across different demographics.

2. Income and Region Analysis: Understanding the income levels and geographical regions of bike buyers was essential to tailor marketing strategies and target potential customers more effectively. We analyzed the dataset to determine the income distribution and identify regions with a higher demand for bikes.

3. Influence of Car and House Ownership, and Presence of Children: We explored whether the ownership of cars and houses, as well as having children, influenced the decision to purchase a bike. This information provided valuable insights into the factors influencing the bike-buying behavior of customers.

Conclusion: By conducting a comprehensive analysis and leveraging the power of Microsoft Excel, we were able to gain deep insights into the bike sales data. The project shed light on the preferences of different demographic groups, the impact of income and region on bike purchases, and the influence of car and house ownership, as well as having children. These findings can be instrumental in formulating marketing strategies, enhancing customer engagement, and optimizing business operations for the bike sale shop.

Data Professional Survey Breakdown

In this project, we embarked on a data analysis journey by importing a CSV survey from Kaggle. The survey was designed to capture the perspectives of individuals working in data-related careers. Using this dataset, we aimed to create an interactive dashboard in Power BI that would provide valuable insights into various aspects of the data professional landscape.

Data Import and Preparation: The first step involved importing the CSV survey data into Power BI. We carefully examined the data to understand its structure and format. We then performed data cleaning and preprocessing to handle missing values, remove duplicates, and ensure data consistency. This crucial step laid the foundation for accurate and meaningful analysis.

Dashboard Creation in Power BI: With the data prepared and cleaned, we designed an intuitive and interactive dashboard using Power BI. The dashboard was crafted to present key findings and patterns in the data in a visually appealing manner. By utilizing various visualization elements such as charts, graphs, and tables, we aimed to make the insights easily understandable and actionable.

Key Analysis Areas: The analysis focused on uncovering several critical aspects of the data professional landscape: 1. Preferred Programming Languages: We investigated the programming languages that most data professionals use or prefer in their work. Understanding the dominant programming languages in this domain can help individuals and organizations align their skill sets and technological investments accordingly.

2. Average Salary by Job Title: Salary is a pivotal aspect when considering career choices. We analyzed the data to determine the average salary based on different job titles within the data industry. This information can assist both job seekers and employers in making informed decisions about compensation and remuneration packages.

3. Geographic Distribution: Exploring the countries in which data professionals live and work offered insights into the global distribution of talent in this field. This knowledge is vital for companies seeking to expand their operations or target specific regions for recruitment.

4. Career Challenges: Understanding the difficulties faced by individuals trying to break into the data industry can help guide educational institutions and employers in providing relevant training and support to aspiring data professionals.

5. Work-Life Balance: The survey's exploration of work-life balance shed light on the overall job satisfaction and well-being of data professionals. This knowledge can guide organizations in fostering a healthy and conducive work environment.
